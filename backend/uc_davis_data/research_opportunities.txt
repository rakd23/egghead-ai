UC DAVIS RESEARCH OPPORTUNITIES

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH

WHY DO RESEARCH?
- Hands-on experience in your field
- Grad school applications (almost required for PhD programs)
- Build relationships with professors (letters of recommendation)
- Contribute to published research
- Paid or for academic credit
- Students say: One of the best experiences at UC Davis

FINDING RESEARCH POSITIONS

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH CENTER (URC)
- Location: South Hall 1229
- Email: urc@ucdavis.edu
- Resources: Database of opportunities, workshops, funding info
- Students say: Start here if you're new to research

HOW TO FIND A LAB
1. Look at professor's research on department websites
2. Read some of their recent papers
3. Email professor expressing genuine interest
4. Attach your resume/transcript
5. Be prepared for no response or rejection - keep trying!
6. Students say: Persistence pays off, professors appreciate genuine interest

BEST TIME TO START
- Freshman/sophomore year ideal
- Junior year still good
- Even seniors can do research
- Summer is often easier to start (less coursework)
- Students say: Earlier the better for building skills

EMAIL TEMPLATE FOR PROFESSORS
Subject: Undergraduate Research Interest in [specific research topic]

Dear Professor [Name],

I am a [year] [major] student interested in your research on [specific topic]. I have taken [relevant courses] and am particularly interested in [specific aspect of their work]. I would like to inquire about potential undergraduate research opportunities in your lab.

I am available [hours/week] and can start [when]. I have attached my resume and transcript for your review.

Thank you for your consideration.
[Your name]

Students say: Be specific about their research, don't send generic emails

RESEARCH FOR CREDIT

COURSE NUMBERS
- 92, 192, 199 (varies by department)
- Usually 2-5 units
- 3-4 hours of work per unit per week
- Can count toward major requirements (check with advisor)
- Students say: Good option if you need units and can't afford unpaid research

PAID RESEARCH POSITIONS
- Some labs have funding to pay students
- Usually $15-18/hour
- Work-study sometimes accepted
- More competitive than volunteer positions
- Students say: Ask about pay upfront, but don't let unpaid stop you from good experience

RESEARCH PROGRAMS & FUNDING

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH GRANTS
- Provost's Undergraduate Fellowship (PUF): Up to $2,500 for summer research
- Mentorships for Undergraduate Research in Agriculture, Letters and Science (MURALS): Summer stipend + housing
- BUSP (Biology Undergraduate Scholars Program): For biology majors
- Students say: Apply early, deadlines in winter/spring quarter

SUMMER RESEARCH PROGRAMS

RESEARCH EXPERIENCES FOR UNDERGRADUATES (REU)
- National NSF-funded programs
- At universities across the US
- Summer programs (8-10 weeks)
- Paid stipend + housing + travel
- Competitive but worth applying
- Deadlines: Usually January-February
- Students say: Great way to explore grad schools, fully funded

UC DAVIS SUMMER RESEARCH PROGRAMS
- Various department-specific programs
- Often for underrepresented students
- Paid positions
- Check department websites

PRESENTING RESEARCH

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH CONFERENCE (URC)
- Annual spring conference
- Present your research via poster or talk
- Great resume builder
- Open to all undergrads doing research
- Students say: Less intimidating than professional conferences, supportive environment

NATIONAL CONFERENCES
- Some labs send undergrads to professional conferences
- Travel often funded by lab or department
- Amazing networking opportunity
- Students say: Highlight of undergrad experience

PUBLISHING RESEARCH
- Some undergrads get on papers
- Takes time (often years)
- Not necessary but impressive for grad school
- Students say: Don't expect it, but it's a great bonus

RESEARCH BY FIELD

STEM RESEARCH
- Biology: Molecular biology, ecology, evolution, marine biology
- Chemistry: Organic, inorganic, analytical, biochemistry
- Physics: Many labs, some computational
- Engineering: Multiple departments, often hands-on projects
- Computer Science: AI, machine learning, cybersecurity
- Students say: STEM labs have most opportunities, often paid

SOCIAL SCIENCES RESEARCH
- Psychology: Cognitive, developmental, social psychology labs
- Economics: Research assistant positions with professors
- Political Science: Data analysis, policy research
- Students say: Often involves data analysis, surveys, literature reviews

HUMANITIES RESEARCH
- History: Archival research, historical analysis
- English: Literary analysis, digital humanities
- Art History: Museum work, curatorial projects
- Students say: More independent work, fewer "lab" opportunities but still valuable

AGRICULTURE RESEARCH
- UC Davis strength
- Plant sciences, animal science, viticulture, food science
- Often field work involved
- Students say: Unique opportunities here, can work with farms/vineyards

GETTING LETTERS OF RECOMMENDATION
- Research advisors write strongest letters
- Work in lab for at least 2 quarters before asking
- Give at least 1 month notice
- Provide resume, transcript, info about what you're applying for
- Students say: This is one of the main reasons to do research

KEY TIPS FROM STUDENTS
- Start early (freshman/sophomore year)
- Email MANY professors, expect lots of rejection
- Show genuine interest in their specific research
- Be reliable and communicate well
- Ask questions and show enthusiasm
- Commit to at least 2 quarters (ideally longer)
- Don't just do it for resume - pick something interesting to you
- Balance with coursework - don't overcommit
