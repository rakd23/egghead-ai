UC DAVIS FINANCIAL AID & MONEY RESOURCES

FINANCIAL AID OFFICE

LOCATION & CONTACT
- Location: Dutton Hall
- Phone: (530) 752-2396
- Email: finaid@ucdavis.edu
- Walk-in hours: Check website (vary by quarter)

FINANCIAL AID TYPES

FAFSA
- File every year by March 2 priority deadline
- Required for federal aid, Cal Grants, most scholarships
- Don't miss the deadline!
- Students say: File early, financial aid packages come out sooner

CAL GRANTS
- California residents only
- Must file FAFSA by deadline
- Cal Grant A: Tuition coverage
- Cal Grant B: Tuition + living expenses

SCHOLARSHIPS
- Apply through ScholarshipUniverse portal
- New scholarships added regularly
- External scholarships: Many available
- Students say: Apply to many, even small ones add up

WORK-STUDY
- Part-time jobs on campus
- Awarded through FAFSA
- Work around your class schedule
- Students say: Great way to earn money and gain experience

EMERGENCY AID

AGGIE GRANT PROGRAM
- Emergency financial assistance
- For unexpected expenses
- Applications reviewed quickly
- Food, housing, medical emergencies

AGGIE COMPASS BASIC NEEDS CENTER
- Location: Memorial Union Lower Level
- Free food pantry
- Emergency housing support
- Financial coaching
- Students say: Don't be embarrassed to use it - it's there to help

MONEY MANAGEMENT

FINANCIAL LITERACY WORKSHOPS
- Through Aggie Compass
- Budgeting basics
- Student loan management
- Credit education

STUDENT JOBS

ON-CAMPUS JOBS
- Check Handshake for postings
- Library, dining halls, rec centers
- Research assistant positions
- Office jobs in departments
- Students say: On-campus jobs are understanding of class schedules

WORK-STUDY POSITIONS
- Priority if you have work-study award
- Many departments hire work-study students

TEXTBOOK COSTS

SAVING MONEY ON BOOKS
- Rent instead of buy
- Buy used at bookstore
- Check Amazon, Chegg
- Library reserves (some textbooks available)
- Share with classmates
- Students say: Never buy before first day - professor might not use it

COST OF LIVING TIPS
- Cook meals instead of eating out
- Use meal plan efficiently
- Bike instead of car (saves gas/parking)
- Buy used furniture from graduating students
- Shop at farmers market (cheaper produce)
- Students say: Davis is expensive but manageable with budgeting
