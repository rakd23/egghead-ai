UC DAVIS JOBS & INTERNSHIPS

CAREER CENTER

LOCATION & SERVICES
- Location: Mrak Hall
- Phone: (530) 752-2855
- Services: Job search help, resume reviews, interview prep, career counseling
- Drop-in hours and appointments available
- Students say: Really helpful for resume feedback and mock interviews

HANDSHAKE
- UC Davis job/internship portal
- Access through career center website
- Thousands of job postings
- On-campus jobs, internships, full-time positions
- Set up job alerts for your interests
- Students say: Check it daily, new postings added constantly

ON-CAMPUS JOBS

WORK-STUDY POSITIONS
- Priority if you have federal work-study award
- Library, dining halls, research labs, administrative offices
- Flexible around class schedules
- Pay: Usually minimum wage to $16/hour
- Students say: Great for resume building and convenience

NON WORK-STUDY JOBS
- Many departments hire without work-study requirement
- Check Handshake for openings
- Student assistant positions
- Event staff, campus tours, recreation centers
- Students say: Apply early in the quarter, positions fill fast

POPULAR ON-CAMPUS EMPLOYERS
- Memorial Union (event staff, building management)
- UC Davis Library (circulation desk, shelving)
- Dining Services (cashier, food prep, servers)
- ARC (front desk, lifeguards, fitness instructors)
- Academic departments (office assistants)
- Admissions (tour guides, student ambassadors)

RESEARCH ASSISTANT POSITIONS
- Work in professor's lab
- Usually 10-20 hours/week
- Can be paid or for credit
- Great for grad school applications
- Students say: Email professors directly, show genuine interest in their research

TEACHING ASSISTANT (TA) POSITIONS
- Usually for upper-division and grad students
- Help grade, hold office hours, sometimes teach discussion sections
- Pay: $15-20/hour typically
- Students say: Looks great on resume, good teaching experience

INTERNSHIPS

FINDING INTERNSHIPS
- Handshake (primary resource)
- Career fairs (fall and spring)
- Department-specific career events
- LinkedIn
- Company websites directly
- Students say: Start looking early, best internships filled by November/December

SUMMER INTERNSHIPS
- Most competitive internships are summer
- Applications open fall/winter
- STEM internships often pay well ($20-40/hour)
- Some provide housing stipends
- Students say: Apply to many, expect lots of rejections

INTERNSHIP FOR CREDIT
- Many majors allow internships for units
- Talk to your major advisor
- Requirements vary by department
- Students say: Good way to get units while interning

UNPAID INTERNSHIPS
- More common in nonprofits, government, some fields
- Can still get academic credit
- Consider carefully if you need income
- Students say: Only take if you can afford it or get housing support

POPULAR INTERNSHIP SECTORS
- Sacramento government (close by, state capitol)
- Bay Area tech companies (1.5 hours away)
- Healthcare (UC Davis Health, Kaiser)
- Agriculture/biotechnology (big in region)
- Consulting, finance, marketing

CAREER FAIRS

INTERNSHIP & CAREER FAIR
- Fall and spring quarters
- Hundreds of employers
- Bring resumes
- Dress professionally
- Students say: Great for networking, some on-site interviews

MAJOR-SPECIFIC FAIRS
- Engineering career fair
- Agriculture career fair
- Life sciences career fair
- Students say: Less crowded than main fair, more targeted opportunities

NETWORKING

LINKEDIN
- Connect with alumni
- Follow companies
- Join UC Davis groups
- Students say: Alumni are usually willing to help fellow Aggies

ALUMNI NETWORK
- UC Davis has strong alumni network
- Alumni mentorship programs available
- Career center can connect you
- Students say: Don't be afraid to reach out, alumni want to help

INFORMATIONAL INTERVIEWS
- Reach out to professionals in your field
- 20-30 minute conversations
- Learn about their career path
- Can lead to job opportunities
- Students say: Best way to learn about careers and make connections

INTERNSHIP PROGRAMS

UCDC (UC DAVIS IN DC)
- Spend a quarter in Washington DC
- Internship + coursework
- Government, policy, nonprofit internships
- Housing provided
- Students say: Amazing experience, competitive to get in

UC SACRAMENTO CENTER
- Quarter in Sacramento
- State government internships
- Less competitive than UCDC
- Students say: Great for political science, public policy majors

CAREER RESOURCES

RESUME & COVER LETTER HELP
- Drop-in resume reviews at career center
- Online resources and templates
- Students say: Get multiple people to review before applying

MOCK INTERVIEWS
- Practice interviews with career counselors
- Feedback on answers and presentation
- Helps with nerves
- Students say: Do at least one before real interviews

SALARY NEGOTIATION WORKSHOPS
- Learn to negotiate offers
- Know your worth
- Students say: Wish I'd done this before accepting first offer

KEY TIPS FROM STUDENTS
- Start looking for summer internships in fall
- Apply to way more positions than you think you need
- Network, network, network
- Don't just apply online - go to career fairs
- Keep your resume updated on Handshake
- GPA matters for some employers, but experience matters more
- Don't give up after rejections - everyone gets them
