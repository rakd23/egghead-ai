UC DAVIS MENTAL HEALTH & WELLNESS RESOURCES(link:https://mentalhealth.ucdavis.edu/resources)

STUDENT HEALTH AND COUNSELING SERVICES (SHCS)
- Location: North Hall Addition
- Phone: (530) 752-2349
- Services: Mental health counseling, psychiatric services, wellness workshops
- Crisis support available
- Free for registered students
- Hours: Monday-Friday, 8am-5pm
- Students say: Counselors are caring, but appointments can take a few weeks

CAPS (COUNSELING AND PSYCHOLOGICAL SERVICES)
- Part of SHCS
- Individual counseling sessions
- Group therapy options
- Crisis intervention
- Workshops on stress management, anxiety, depression
- Students can get referred to off-campus providers if needed

LET'S TALK
- Free, informal drop-in consultations with counselors
- No appointment needed
- 15-20 minute sessions
- Multiple locations across campus
- Good for: Quick questions, deciding if you need ongoing counseling
- Students say: Great for when you just need to talk to someone

AGGIE MENTAL HEALTH FIRST AID
- Peer-to-peer support program
- Trained student volunteers
- Help connecting to resources
- Non-crisis support

CRISIS RESOURCES
- SHCS Crisis Line: (530) 752-2349 (24/7)
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- Campus Police (emergency): 911 or (530) 752-1230

PEER SUPPORT
- Active Minds student organization
- Mental health awareness and advocacy
- Events and workshops throughout the year

WELLNESS RESOURCES
- Aggie Recharge Room (MU): Quiet space for mental breaks
- Meditation and mindfulness apps available through SHCS
- Wellness Coaching available

MINDFULNESS & MEDITATION
- Free meditation classes at the ARC
- Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) workshops
- Yoga classes available

KEY POINTS:
- All mental health services at SHCS are free for students
- Confidential services
- Can be hard to get appointments during peak times (midterms, finals)
- Don't wait until crisis - reach out early
